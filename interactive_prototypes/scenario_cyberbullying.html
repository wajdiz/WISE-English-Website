What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is the use of digital communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen 24 hours a day, reach a person even when alone, and quickly reach a wide audience.

The following scenarios illustrate common forms of cyberbullying and provide guidance on how to respond effectively.

Scenario 1: Repeated Negative Comments

Potential Impact:

This type of repeated negative targeting can lead to:

  • Decreased self-esteem and confidence
  • Reluctance to share creative work
  • Anxiety about school and facing peers
  • Feelings of isolation if others see the comments but don't intervene

Response Options:

Recommended: Block and Report

Block Jason to prevent further comments and report the harassment to the platform. Save screenshots as evidence.

Recommended: Talk to a Trusted Adult

Share what's happening with a parent, teacher, or school counselor who can provide support and potentially intervene.

Not Recommended: Respond with Anger

Responding with insults or anger might temporarily feel good but typically escalates the situation and provides the reaction the bully is seeking.

Expert Tip:

Document the bullying by taking screenshots that include timestamps and usernames. This evidence can be important if the situation requires intervention from school administrators or, in severe cases, law enforcement.

Scenario 2: Group Chat Exclusion

Messaging App

Miguel, 13, discovers his friends have created a group chat called "Everyone Except Miguel" where they share jokes and make plans without him. Sometimes they screenshot conversations from this group and send them to Miguel to show him what he's missing.

Tyler: 5:15 PM

Hey Miguel, look what you're missing out on! [Screenshot of group chat planning a weekend hangout]

Miguel: 5:17 PM

Why didn't you guys invite me?

Tyler: 5:18 PM

It's just a joke, don't be so sensitive ðŸ™„

Tyler: 5:20 PM

Everyone in the group thinks it's funny

Potential Impact:

This form of exclusionary bullying can cause:

  • Feelings of rejection and isolation
  • Damaged self-worth and confidence
  • Trust issues in future friendships
  • Anxiety about social situations

Response Options:

Recommended: Express Feelings Calmly

Clearly communicate how their actions make you feel: "When you exclude me and then show me what I'm missing, it hurts my feelings and makes me feel isolated."

Recommended: Seek New Social Connections

Focus on building friendships with people who value and include you, whether through clubs, activities, or other social groups.

Not Recommended: Pretending It Doesn't Bother You

Ignoring the problem without addressing it or seeking support can lead to increased feelings of isolation and allow the behavior to continue.

Expert Tip:

Exclusion is a form of bullying that's sometimes dismissed as "just a joke" or "not that serious." However, intentional and repeated exclusion can be just as harmful as more direct forms of bullying. It's important to recognize this as a legitimate form of cyberbullying that deserves attention and intervention.

Scenario 3: Impersonation

Social Media Platform

Aisha, 15, discovers someone has created a fake profile using her name and photo. The account is posting embarrassing and offensive content that makes it look like she is saying inappropriate things.

Jordan: 7:32 PM

Aisha, what's with all those awful posts you're making? I thought you were better than that.

Aisha: 7:35 PM

What posts? I haven't posted anything today.

Jordan: 7:36 PM

Check your profile. You've been posting all day saying really mean things about people in our class.

Aisha: 7:38 PM

That's not me! Someone must have created a fake account with my name and photo. Can you send me a link to it?

Potential Impact:

This type of impersonation can lead to:

  • Damage to reputation and relationships
  • Being blamed for things you didn't say or do
  • Feelings of violation and loss of control
  • Anxiety about what might be posted next

Response Options:

Recommended: Report the Fake Account

Report the impersonation to the platform immediately. Most social media platforms have specific procedures for reporting fake accounts.

Recommended: Inform Your Network

Post on your real accounts to let friends know about the fake profile and ask them to report it too.

Recommended: Involve Parents and School

This type of impersonation can quickly escalate and cause significant harm. Involve trusted adults who can help address the situation.

Expert Tip:

Impersonation is a serious form of cyberbullying that can sometimes violate terms of service and even laws in some jurisdictions. Document everything by taking screenshots of the fake profile and any harmful posts. This documentation will be valuable when reporting to platforms, school officials, or in some cases, law enforcement.
