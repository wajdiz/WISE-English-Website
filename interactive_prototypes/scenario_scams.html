Understanding, recognizing, and responding to online scams

What are Online Scams?

Online scams are deceptive schemes designed to trick people into giving away money, personal information, or access to their accounts. Scammers often create a sense of urgency, excitement, or fear to manipulate victims into making quick decisions without careful consideration. Young people can be particularly vulnerable to certain types of scams, especially those involving gaming, social media, or trendy products.

The following scenarios illustrate different forms of online scams and provide guidance on how to recognize and respond effectively.

Scenario 1: Free Gift Card Scam

Social Media Platform

Noah, 14, receives a direct message from what appears to be a popular gaming company offering free gift cards in exchange for completing a survey and sharing his account information.

GamezWorld_Official: 4:15 PM

CONGRATULATIONS! You've been randomly selected to receive a $100 GamezWorld gift card! ðŸŽ® We're giving away 50 gift cards to loyal players like you. Complete this quick survey and provide your account details to claim your prize: [suspicious link]

Noah: 4:20 PM

Really? That's awesome! How did I get selected?

GamezWorld_Official: 4:22 PM

You were chosen based on your activity on our platform! But hurry, this offer expires in 30 minutes! Click the link now to claim your gift card before someone else gets it!

Noah: 4:25 PM

Wait, I'm checking the official GamezWorld social media accounts and I don't see anything about a gift card giveaway. And your account name is slightly different from their official one...

GamezWorld_Official: 4:27 PM

This is a special promotion for select users only! That's why it's not public. You only have 25 minutes left! Don't miss out on your FREE $100!

Potential Impact:

Falling for gift card scams can lead to:

  • Theft of personal information and account credentials
  • Unauthorized purchases using your account or payment methods
  • Installation of malware if suspicious links are clicked
  • Loss of access to gaming or social media accounts
  • Financial loss if scammers gain access to payment information

Response Options:

Recommended: Verify Through Official Channels

Check the company's official website or verified social media accounts to confirm if they're actually running such a promotion.

Recommended: Report the Account

Report the suspicious account to the platform for impersonating a company and attempting to scam users.

Not Recommended: Clicking Suspicious Links

Never click on links from unknown or suspicious sources, even if they promise free rewards or gifts.

Expert Tip:

Legitimate companies rarely contact individuals out of the blue to offer free gifts or rewards. They also never ask for account credentials or personal information through direct messages. Be especially wary of messages that create a sense of urgency ("offer expires in 30 minutes") or exclusivity ("special promotion for select users only") as these are classic pressure tactics used by scammers to prevent you from taking time to verify their claims.

Scenario 2: Account Recovery Scam

Gaming Platform

Sophia, 16, receives a message from someone claiming to be a platform administrator, saying her gaming account has been flagged for suspicious activity and she needs to provide her login credentials to verify her identity and prevent account deletion.

Admin_Support_Team: Tuesday, 7:30 PM

URGENT NOTICE: Your account has been flagged for suspicious activity and potential Terms of Service violations. You must verify your identity within 24 hours or your account will be permanently deleted. Reply with your account email and password to begin the verification process.

Sophia: Tuesday, 7:35 PM

What? I haven't done anything wrong! What suspicious activity?

Admin_Support_Team: Tuesday, 7:37 PM

Our system detected multiple login attempts from different locations. This is a standard security check. We need your login credentials to verify you are the legitimate account owner. This is the only way to prevent permanent account deletion.

Sophia: Tuesday, 7:40 PM

I don't think real admins would ask for my password. I'm going to check the official support page instead.

Admin_Support_Team: Tuesday, 7:42 PM

This is a special emergency procedure. If you don't comply immediately, we cannot guarantee your account will be recoverable. You have 23 hours remaining before permanent deletion.

Potential Impact:

Falling for account recovery scams can lead to:

  • Complete loss of access to your gaming or social media accounts
  • Theft of virtual items, in-game currency, or purchased content
  • Scammers using your account to scam others in your friend list
  • Exposure of personal information stored in your account
  • Financial loss if payment methods are saved to the account

Response Options:

Recommended: Contact Official Support

Ignore the message and contact the platform's official support through their website or app to verify if there are any actual issues with your account.

Recommended: Report the Scam Attempt

Report the account that sent the message to the platform for impersonating administrators and attempting to steal account credentials.

Not Recommended: Sharing Login Credentials

Never share your password, verification codes, or other account credentials with anyone, even if they claim to be platform administrators or support staff.

Expert Tip:

Legitimate platform administrators and support staff will never ask for your full password or login credentials. If there's a genuine issue with your account, they have internal tools to address it without needing your password. Always access support services through the official website or app, not through messages you receive. Remember that scammers often create a false sense of urgency to pressure you into making hasty decisions.

Scenario 3: Fake Marketplace Listing

Online Marketplace

Ethan, 17, finds an incredible deal on a high-end gaming console on an online marketplace. The seller is offering it for 70% below retail price but requires payment through a non-standard method before shipping.

Marketplace Listing: Friday, 3:15 PM

BRAND NEW Gaming Console XS Pro - $150 (Retail $499)

Still in sealed box. Unwanted gift. Need to sell quickly. First come, first served. No returns.

[Stock photo of gaming console]

Ethan: Friday, 3:30 PM

Hi, I'm interested in the gaming console. Is it still available? That's an amazing price!

Seller: Friday, 3:35 PM

Yes still available but I have many people interested. If you want it, you need to send payment now to secure it. I can't use the marketplace payment system due to account issues. Send money via gift card codes or wire transfer and I'll ship it tomorrow.

Ethan: Friday, 3:40 PM

Can I see more photos of the actual console you have? And why can't we use the regular marketplace payment system?

Seller: Friday, 3:45 PM

Can't take more photos right now as it's packaged for shipping. I have 3 other buyers ready to pay. If you want it, you need to send payment in the next hour. I only accept gift cards or wire transfer because the marketplace holds funds for too long.

Potential Impact:

Falling for marketplace scams can lead to:

  • Financial loss with no way to recover the money
  • No product received after payment is sent
  • Receiving counterfeit or broken items
  • Exposure of personal information if shipping details are provided
  • Potential for identity theft if too much personal information is shared

Response Options:

Recommended: Use Platform Protection

Only make purchases through the marketplace's official payment system, which typically offers buyer protection and dispute resolution.

Recommended: Research Market Value

Be skeptical of deals that seem too good to be true. Research the typical resale value of items to recognize unrealistic discounts.

Not Recommended: Alternative Payment Methods

Avoid payment methods that offer no protection or recourse, such as wire transfers, cryptocurrency, gift card codes, or money transfer apps to strangers.

Expert Tip:

When shopping on online marketplaces, remember that scammers often create a sense of competition ("3 other buyers ready to pay") and urgency ("need to send payment in the next hour") to pressure you into making quick decisions. Legitimate sellers will generally be willing to provide additional photos of the actual item, answer questions about the product, and use the platform's standard payment methods. If a seller insists on payment methods that offer no buyer protection, it's almost always a scam.
