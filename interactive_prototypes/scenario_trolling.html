Understanding, recognizing, and responding to trolling

What is Trolling?

Trolling is the deliberate act of making random, offensive, or controversial comments online with the intent to provoke an emotional reaction or disrupt normal on-topic discussion. Unlike some other forms of online toxicity, trolling is specifically aimed at causing confusion, frustration, or anger rather than targeting a specific person.

The following scenarios illustrate different forms of trolling and provide guidance on how to respond effectively.

Scenario 1: Derailing a Discussion

Online Forum

A group of students is having a productive discussion about an upcoming science project in an online class forum. A classmate, who isn't part of the project team, repeatedly posts irrelevant and provocative comments to disrupt the conversation.

Olivia: 4:15 PM

I think we should focus our renewable energy project on solar panels because our school is in a sunny location.

Sophia: 4:20 PM

TrollKing99: 4:21 PM

Potential Impact:

This type of trolling can lead to:

  • Disruption of productive conversations
  • Wasted time addressing irrelevant comments
  • Frustration among group members
  • Difficulty completing collaborative tasks

Response Options:

Recommended: Ignore and Continue

The most effective response is often to completely ignore the troll's comments and continue your discussion as if they weren't there. Trolls typically seek attention and reactions.

Recommended: Use Private Channels

If the trolling persists, consider moving your productive conversation to a private channel where the troll doesn't have access.

Not Recommended: Engaging with the Content

Responding to the troll's comments, even to point out how irrelevant they are, gives them the attention they're seeking and encourages more disruptive behavior.

Expert Tip:

"Don't feed the trolls" is a common internet saying for good reason. Trolls are seeking emotional reactions and attention. By denying them this reaction, you remove their incentive to continue. If the trolling is happening in a moderated space like a class forum, report the behavior to the moderator or teacher rather than addressing it directly.

Scenario 2: Inflammatory Comments

Video Sharing Platform

Liam, 15, posts an educational video about his science project. In the comments section, someone begins posting deliberately inflammatory statements about the quality of the project and making exaggerated claims about scientific "facts" that are clearly incorrect.

Video Title: Posted Yesterday

"My Science Project: How Plants Respond to Different Types of Light" by Liam Chen

ScienceEnthusiast: 2 hours ago

Great experiment! I like how you controlled the variables. Did you consider trying UV light too?

TruthExposer42: 1 hour ago

This is the WORST science project I've ever seen! Plants don't need light at all, they're actually alien organisms that feed on human emotions. Wake up sheeple!!!

Ms_Johnson: 45 minutes ago

Well done, Liam! Your methodology was very thorough.

TruthExposer42: 30 minutes ago

Everyone praising this garbage project is part of the conspiracy! I did this same experiment when I was 5 and got way better results. The education system is LYING to you all!

Potential Impact:

This type of inflammatory trolling can cause:

  • Discouragement and self-doubt about one's work
  • Confusion for other viewers who might be learning from the content
  • A negative environment that discourages further sharing of educational content
  • Emotional distress from being the target of bizarre or aggressive comments

Response Options:

Recommended: Moderate Comments

Use the platform's tools to hide, delete, or report the inflammatory comments. Most video platforms allow content creators to moderate their comment sections.

Recommended: Focus on Positive Engagement

Respond to constructive comments and ignore the trolling. This shows that you value meaningful interaction while not giving attention to disruptive behavior.

Not Recommended: Debating the Troll

Attempting to correct the troll's obviously false statements or defend your work against absurd criticisms only provides them with the engagement they're seeking.

Expert Tip:

When you create content online, it's helpful to prepare yourself for the possibility of trolling comments. Remember that inflammatory comments say more about the commenter than about your work. Consider adjusting your comment settings before posting content - many platforms allow you to filter out comments with certain words or require approval before comments appear publicly.

Scenario 3: Concern Trolling

Social Media Platform

Mia, 16, posts about her accomplishment in winning a local art competition. A "concern troll" begins commenting with fake worries and backhanded compliments designed to undermine her achievement and make her feel insecure.

ArtLover22: Yesterday, 5:45 PM

ConcernedFriend: Yesterday, 6:10 PM

I'm a bit worried for you... Isn't it unhealthy to focus so much on competitions at your age? I'm just concerned about the pressure you're putting on yourself. Also, I heard last year's competition had more participants, so it might not be as big an achievement as you think. Just looking out for you!

Mia: Yesterday, 6:25 PM

Thanks for the concern, but I'm really happy with this achievement. It was a tough competition with lots of talented artists.

ConcernedFriend: Yesterday, 6:30 PM

I just think it's important to be realistic. Your style is very... unique, which might not translate to success in the real art world. I'd hate for you to be disappointed later. Have you considered a more practical career path? Just trying to help!

Potential Impact:

Concern trolling can lead to:

  • Self-doubt and diminished joy in achievements
  • Confusion about whether the "concern" is legitimate
  • Feeling pressured to defend or justify accomplishments
  • Reluctance to share future achievements

Response Options:

Recommended: Brief Acknowledgment and Redirection

A simple "Thanks for your input" before returning to positive interactions can deny the troll the extended engagement they seek.

Recommended: Block and Move On

If the concern trolling persists, blocking the user prevents further negative interactions without creating drama.

Not Recommended: Detailed Defense

Providing detailed explanations or defenses of your accomplishments plays into the troll's strategy of making you feel like you need to justify yourself.

Expert Tip:

Concern trolling is particularly insidious because it disguises negativity as helpful advice or worry. A good way to identify concern trolling is to ask yourself: "Would a true friend or supporter express this concern in this way, in this public forum?" Genuine concerns from people who care about you are typically expressed privately and constructively, not as public comments that undermine your achievements.
