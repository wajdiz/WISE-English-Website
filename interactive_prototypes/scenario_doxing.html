Doxing Scenarios

Understanding, recognizing, and responding to doxing

What is Doxing?

Doxing (sometimes spelled "doxxing") is the act of publicly revealing previously private personal information about an individual or organization, usually through the internet. This information may include real name, home address, phone number, email address, workplace, family members' information, and more.

Doxing is particularly harmful because it can bridge the gap between online harassment and real-world danger. The following scenarios illustrate different forms of doxing and provide guidance on how to respond effectively.

Scenario 1: Gaming Dispute Escalation

Online Gaming Platform

Ethan, 16, defeats another player in an online competitive game. The player becomes angry and threatens to "find" Ethan. Later, Ethan discovers the player has posted his real name, school, and city in the game's public chat.

Game Chat: 8:45 PM

Ethan (GamerX92): Good game!

Game Chat: 8:46 PM

DestroyerZ: You cheated. Nobody beats me like that without hacks.

Game Chat: 8:47 PM

Ethan (GamerX92): No cheats, just practice. Want to play again?

Game Chat: 8:48 PM

DestroyerZ: I'm going to find you. I know how to get people's info.

Game Chat: 9:12 PM

DestroyerZ: Hey everyone, GamerX92 is Ethan Thompson from Lincoln High School in Portland. Let's all report this cheater.

Potential Impact:

This type of doxing can lead to:

  • Fear for physical safety when strangers know your location
  • Harassment extending to real-life settings like school
  • Loss of privacy and sense of security
  • Anxiety about playing games or participating in online activities

Response Options:

Recommended: Report to Platform Immediately

Most gaming platforms have strict policies against doxing. Report the user and include screenshots of the messages containing your personal information.

Recommended: Inform Parents/Guardians

This situation involves potential safety concerns and should be shared with trusted adults who can help assess the risk and take appropriate action.

Not Recommended: Retaliating with Counter-Doxing

Attempting to find and share the other person's information escalates the situation, may be illegal, and puts you at risk of account bans or other consequences.

Expert Tip:

Review your online presence to see what information might be publicly available. Sometimes doxers compile information from multiple sources like social media profiles, forum posts, and usernames that are used across different platforms. Consider using different usernames for different services and checking privacy settings on all your accounts.

Scenario 2: Social Media Disagreement

Public Forum

Zoe, 17, posts a comment expressing her opinion on a controversial topic in a public forum. A group of users who disagree with her view begin digging into her online presence and compile a post containing her full name, parents' names, home address, and phone number.

Forum Thread: 3:15 PM

Zoe (@ZoeThinks): I think we need to consider both sides of this issue before jumping to conclusions...

Forum Thread: 3:22 PM

@TruthTeller45: @ZoeThinks People like you are the problem. There are no "both sides" to this.

Forum Thread: 3:45 PM

@NoCompromise: I found her Instagram. Her real name is Zoe Martinez. Let's see what else we can find.

Private Message: 4:30 PM

Anonymous User: We know where you live, Zoe. 1234 Oak Street. Maybe we should let your parents Robert and Maria know what you're saying online. Their number is (555) 123-4567, right?

Potential Impact:

This coordinated doxing can cause:

  • Immediate safety concerns for the individual and their family
  • Fear of stalking or physical confrontation
  • Harassment extending to family members
  • Silencing effect on future expression of opinions

Response Options:

Recommended: Document Everything

Take screenshots of all posts containing your personal information, including usernames, dates, and times.

Recommended: Report to Platform and Consider Law Enforcement

Report the doxing to the platform. In cases where there are explicit or implied threats, consider reporting to local law enforcement as well.

Recommended: Enhance Security Measures

Change passwords, enable two-factor authentication on accounts, and consider temporarily making social media accounts private.

Expert Tip:

Doxing that includes home addresses and contact information of minors is taken very seriously by most platforms and may violate laws in many jurisdictions. Don't hesitate to involve parents, school officials, and if necessary, law enforcement when this type of information is shared with malicious intent.

Scenario 3: Unintentional Self-Doxing

Social Media Platform

Noah, 14, regularly posts about his daily life on social media. Without realizing it, his posts over time have revealed his full name, school, home address (through location tags and photos), sports schedule, and family members' information. A stranger uses this information to create a detailed profile about Noah.

Noah's Social Media: Monday, 3:15 PM

First day back at Jefferson Middle School! 8th grade is going to be awesome! [Photo of school entrance]

Noah's Social Media: Wednesday, 5:30 PM

Soccer practice was intense today! Coach Williams is getting us ready for Saturday's game against Westside. [Location tagged: Memorial Park Fields]

Noah's Social Media: Friday, 7:45 PM

Movie night with the family! [Photo showing family members with caption tagging "Mom", "Dad", and "Sister Lily"]

Noah's Social Media: Saturday, 10:20 AM

Just got my new gaming setup! [Photo showing bedroom with house number visible through window]

Potential Impact:

Unintentional self-doxing can lead to:

  • Vulnerability to stalking or predatory behavior
  • Identity theft risks
  • Targeted harassment or scams
  • Privacy violations affecting the entire family

Response Options:

Recommended: Audit and Clean Up Social Media

Review and remove posts that reveal personal information. Turn off location tagging and be more selective about what you share going forward.

Recommended: Update Privacy Settings

Set accounts to private and review friend/follower lists to ensure you know who has access to your information.

Recommended: Create a Family Social Media Plan

Discuss with family members what information is safe to share online and what should remain private.

Expert Tip:

Information mosaic is a technique where seemingly harmless pieces of information shared over time can be compiled to create a detailed profile. Before posting, ask yourself: "What could someone learn about me if they saw all my posts together?" Be especially careful about sharing routine locations (school, practice schedules), home details, and family information.
