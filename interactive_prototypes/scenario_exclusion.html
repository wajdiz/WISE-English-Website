Exclusion Scenarios

Understanding, recognizing, and responding to online exclusion

What is Online Exclusion?

Online exclusion is a form of social rejection where individuals are deliberately left out of online groups, activities, or conversations. This can include being excluded from group chats, online games, social media interactions, or collaborative projects. While sometimes less visible than other forms of online toxicity, exclusion can be equally harmful to mental health and social development.

The following scenarios illustrate different forms of online exclusion and provide guidance on how to respond effectively.

Scenario 1: The Secret Group Chat

Messaging App

Lily, 14, discovers that all of her friends from school have a group chat that she wasn't invited to join. They frequently make plans there and discuss inside jokes that Lily doesn't understand when they talk at school.

Lily: 4:15 PM

Hey Madison, do you want to hang out this weekend? Maybe we could go to the mall?

Madison: 4:20 PM

Sorry, I can't. We're all going to Emma's house on Saturday for a movie night.

Lily: 4:22 PM

Oh, I didn't know about that. Who's going?

Madison: 4:25 PM

Everyone from the group chat. Emma posted about it yesterday.

Lily: 4:27 PM

What group chat? I don't think I'm in it.

Madison: 4:30 PM

Oh... it's just a chat some of us started a while ago. I thought you knew about it. Sorry.

Potential Impact:

This type of exclusion can lead to:

  • Feelings of rejection and isolation
  • Decreased self-esteem and confidence
  • Missing out on social events and opportunities
  • Anxiety about social status and relationships
  • Difficulty trusting friends

Response Options:

Recommended: Express Feelings Directly

Have an honest conversation with a close friend from the group about how being excluded makes you feel. Focus on your feelings rather than blaming or accusing.

Recommended: Expand Your Social Circle

While working on existing friendships, consider joining clubs, activities, or online communities based on your interests to develop additional social connections.

Not Recommended: Creating a Rival Group

Starting your own exclusive group chat to exclude those who excluded you might feel satisfying in the moment but typically escalates social division and doesn't address the underlying issue.

Expert Tip:

Sometimes exclusion happens unintentionally as social groups evolve. When addressing the situation, use "I" statements to express how you feel without making accusations. For example, "I feel left out when plans are made without me" is more effective than "You always exclude me." This approach makes it easier for friends to respond positively rather than becoming defensive.

Scenario 2: Collaborative Project Exclusion

School Online Platform

Carlos, 16, is part of a 5-person team for a class project that uses a collaborative online platform. He notices that the other team members have been working on the project during times they claimed they were "too busy" to meet with him, and they've been making key decisions without his input.

Project Platform: Monday, 7:30 PM

Activity Log: Jessica, Ryan, Aiden, and Sophia edited the project outline and assigned research topics.

Carlos: Tuesday, 3:15 PM

Hey team, I noticed you all met yesterday to work on the project. I thought we agreed to meet on Wednesday when everyone was free?

Jessica: Tuesday, 3:30 PM

We had some free time and decided to get started. We assigned you the conclusion section. Hope that's okay!

Carlos: Tuesday, 3:35 PM

I would have liked to be part of the planning process. I had some ideas for the project structure that I wanted to share.

Ryan: Tuesday, 3:40 PM

We're pretty set on the current structure. Just focus on your part and we'll put everything together.

Potential Impact:

This type of academic exclusion can cause:

  • Feeling devalued and that your contributions don't matter
  • Anxiety about group work and collaborative projects
  • Reduced learning opportunities from the collaborative process
  • Potential impact on academic performance and grades
  • Reluctance to participate in future group projects

Response Options:

Recommended: Request a Group Meeting

Ask for a formal meeting with the entire group to discuss project roles, expectations, and communication. Frame it as wanting to ensure the best outcome for everyone.

Recommended: Involve the Teacher if Necessary

If the exclusion continues after you've tried to address it directly, speak with your teacher about the situation. Most educators want to ensure equitable participation in group projects.

Not Recommended: Doing Minimal Work

While it might be tempting to put in minimal effort when you've been excluded from key decisions, this approach can harm your own grade and learning experience.

Expert Tip:

When addressing exclusion in academic settings, focus on the project outcomes rather than personal feelings. For example, "I believe I could contribute valuable ideas to the introduction section based on my research" is more effective than "It's not fair that you didn't include me." This approach emphasizes your value to the team and keeps the conversation centered on the shared goal of creating a successful project.

Scenario 3: Gaming Clan Exclusion

Online Gaming Platform

Jamal, 15, has been part of an online gaming clan for six months. Recently, he's noticed that other clan members are playing together without inviting him, even when he's online. When he asks to join, they make excuses or ignore his messages.

Gaming Platform: 8:15 PM

Notification: Your clan members DarkWarrior, NightHawk, and EliteSniper are now playing Ranked Match.

Jamal (ShadowBlade): 8:17 PM

Hey guys, I'm online too. Can I join the next match?

DarkWarrior: 8:25 PM

Sorry, we're doing high-level ranked matches right now. Maybe next time.

Jamal (ShadowBlade): 8:27 PM

I'm at the same rank as NightHawk though. I've been practicing a lot.

Gaming Platform: 8:40 PM

Notification: Your clan members DarkWarrior, NightHawk, EliteSniper, and StormRider have started a new match.

Potential Impact:

Exclusion from gaming communities can lead to:

  • Feeling rejected by a community that was previously a source of belonging
  • Missing out on skill development and game progression
  • Loss of social connections and friendships
  • Decreased enjoyment of a previously fun activity
  • Questioning your value and worth as a player and team member

Response Options:

Recommended: Direct Conversation

Reach out to a clan member you're closest with and ask for honest feedback about why you're being excluded. There might be a misunderstanding or specific concern you can address.

Recommended: Explore New Communities

Look for other clans or gaming communities that share your values around inclusion and teamwork. Many gaming communities prioritize positive social experiences.

Not Recommended: Confronting the Group Publicly

Calling out the entire clan in a group chat or public forum can escalate the situation and make reconciliation less likely.

Expert Tip:

Gaming communities can sometimes develop exclusive subgroups based on skill level, play style, or simply friendship dynamics. If you're experiencing exclusion, it's worth considering whether this particular community is the right fit for you. The gaming world is vast, with many communities that actively welcome new members and value inclusion. Your gaming experience should be enjoyable and socially fulfilling, not a source of stress or rejection.
