What is Online Harassment?

Online harassment refers to targeted, persistent, and unwanted behavior directed at a specific person with the intent to alarm, annoy, torment, or cause emotional distress. Unlike some other forms of online toxicity, harassment typically involves repeated actions over time rather than isolated incidents.

The following scenarios illustrate different forms of online harassment and provide guidance on how to respond effectively.

Scenario 1: Persistent Unwanted Contact

Social Media Platform

Alex, 16, politely declined a date request from a classmate, Jordan. Since then, Jordan has been sending multiple messages every day across different platforms, creating new accounts when blocked, and commenting on all of Alex's social media posts.

Jordan: Monday, 3:15 PM

Hey, why aren't you answering my texts? I just want to talk to you.

Alex: Monday, 3:30 PM

I already told you I'm not interested in dating. Please respect my decision.

Jordan: Monday, 3:32 PM

We could just be friends. I'll keep trying until you give me a chance.

Jordan: Monday, 5:45 PM

I saw you were online but you're ignoring me. Why are you being so mean?

Jordan (new account): Tuesday, 7:20 AM

You blocked me? I made a new account just to talk to you. You can't avoid me forever.

Potential Impact:

This type of persistent unwanted contact can lead to:

  • Anxiety and stress when using social media or checking messages
  • Feeling unsafe or constantly monitored
  • Withdrawal from online platforms to avoid the harasser
  • Sleep disturbances and difficulty concentrating
  • Concern about the harassment escalating to in-person contact

Response Options:

Recommended: Clear Boundary Setting

Send one clear message stating that the contact is unwanted and must stop. After this, do not respond to further messages as any response can encourage continued contact.

Recommended: Document Everything

Take screenshots of all unwanted messages, including dates, times, and the platforms they were sent on. This creates a record if you need to escalate the situation.

Recommended: Involve Trusted Adults

Share what's happening with parents, school counselors, or other trusted adults who can help assess the situation and intervene if necessary.

Expert Tip:

Persistent unwanted contact that continues after clear requests to stop can constitute harassment and may violate laws in many jurisdictions. Most social media platforms have specific reporting options for harassment that involves creating multiple accounts to circumvent blocks. When reporting, mention that you've already blocked previous accounts and the person keeps creating new ones to contact you.

Scenario 2: Coordinated Harassment

Online Gaming Community

Tanya, 15, beat a popular player in an online game tournament. The player then encouraged their followers to target Tanya, resulting in dozens of people sending harassing messages, leaving negative comments on her content, and trying to get her banned from the game.

PopularGamer: Friday, 8:30 PM

Just got beaten by this nobody @TanyaGames. She must have been cheating. My followers know what to do... ðŸ˜ˆ

Fan1: Friday, 8:35 PM

@TanyaGames You're going to regret cheating against PopularGamer. We're reporting your account.

Fan2: Friday, 8:40 PM

Everyone go to @TanyaGames stream and dislike it! Let's get her banned!

Fan3: Friday, 9:15 PM

Found her other social media accounts. Let's make sure she never wants to play again.

Private Message: Friday, 10:20 PM

You've received 24 new messages from users you don't follow.

Potential Impact:

Coordinated harassment campaigns can cause:

  • Overwhelming feelings of being attacked from all sides
  • Fear of participating in gaming or online communities
  • Damage to online reputation and social connections
  • Loss of access to platforms if false reports succeed
  • Severe emotional distress from the scale of negativity

Response Options:

Recommended: Lockdown Social Media Temporarily

Set accounts to private, disable comments, or take a brief break from platforms until the initial wave passes. Most coordinated harassment loses momentum after a few days.

Recommended: Report Coordinated Behavior

Report to platforms not just individual harassers but the coordination itself, which violates most platforms' terms of service more severely than individual harassment.

Not Recommended: Public Response

Making public statements addressing the harassment often fuels further targeting and gives harassers the attention and reaction they're seeking.

Expert Tip:

Coordinated harassment often looks more overwhelming than it actually is. Many times, it's a small number of people creating the impression of a larger mob. Platforms are increasingly recognizing "brigading" (coordinated mass harassment) as a serious violation. When reporting, include screenshots of the original call to action that encouraged others to harass you, as this is strong evidence of coordinated behavior.

Scenario 3: Harassment Through Impersonation

School Social Network

David, 14, discovers someone has created a fake profile using his name and photo on a school social network. The impersonator is sending embarrassing messages to classmates and teachers, pretending to be David, causing damage to his reputation and relationships.

Classmate: Wednesday, 4:15 PM

David, why did you send that weird message to everyone in our math group? Not cool, man.

David: Wednesday, 4:17 PM

What message? I didn't send anything to the math group.

Classmate: Wednesday, 4:20 PM

Check your account. You sent a message saying you cheated on the test and copied from Sarah.

David: Wednesday, 4:22 PM

That wasn't me! Someone must have created a fake account with my name. Can you send me a screenshot of the message?

Classmate: Wednesday, 4:25 PM

[Screenshot showing a profile with David's photo but username "David_Thompson2" sending inappropriate messages]

Potential Impact:

Harassment through impersonation can lead to:

  • Damage to reputation and relationships
  • Being blamed or punished for actions you didn't take
  • Confusion and distrust among peers and authority figures
  • Anxiety about what the impersonator might do next
  • Difficulty proving your innocence

Response Options:

Recommended: Immediate Notification

Notify friends, classmates, and teachers about the fake account so they know not to trust messages from it.

Recommended: Report to Platform and School

Report the impersonation to both the platform and school administrators, providing evidence that the account is not yours.

Recommended: Verify Your Identity

If possible, post from your verified accounts confirming the other account is fake and asking people to report it.

Expert Tip:

Impersonation is a particularly harmful form of harassment because it can damage your reputation while simultaneously making it difficult to defend yourself. Most platforms take impersonation very seriously, especially when it involves minors. When reporting, emphasize that the account is pretending to be you without your consent and is sending messages that could harm your reputation or get you in trouble at school.
